Censorship

Cancel culture & social conformity

Censorship on social media is a contested issue. On one hand, platforms regulate harmful or misleading content, but on the other, they are often criticised for removing posts that challenge authority or raise sensitive issues. This raises questions about freedom of expression and who gets to decide what remains visible online.

These tensions contribute to what’s commonly called cancel culture, where public opinion can amplify calls to silence individuals or groups. While sometimes justified as a form of accountability, it can also reinforce conformity, discouraging people from expressing dissenting or creative ideas. The debate continues: is this regulation protecting users, or narrowing public discourse?
