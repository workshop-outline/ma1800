Week 5 — GIF Animation and Meme Culture

This week we are exploring GIF animation and its role in internet culture. GIFs (Graphics Interchange Format) were first introduced in the late 1980s and became a defining part of the early web. Their looping quality and small file size made them perfect for sharing, long before video streaming was widely possible.

In online spaces, GIFs have become more than just moving images. They are used to communicate emotions, share jokes, and create memes. Meme culture has helped GIFs spread rapidly, turning them into a universal internet language. Their popularity also comes from how easy they are to remix and repurpose — reflecting the participatory nature of digital culture.

In this session, we will use GIMP to create simple GIF animations, and then learn how to embed and style them in a web page. These exercises demonstrate how GIFs can shape the look and feel of a site, as well as how they connect to wider cultural practices of looping, repetition, and humour.
