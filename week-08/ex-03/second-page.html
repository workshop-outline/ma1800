The Web Expands

After the first website went live at CERN in 1991, the World Wide Web quickly began to spread beyond the research community. A key moment came in 1993 with the release of NCSA Mosaic, the first widely used graphical web browser developed by Marc Andreessen and his team at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications in Illinois.

Mosaic made the web accessible to non-technical users by supporting images and text on the same page — a feature we now take for granted. Its popularity helped spark a wave of interest in the web, leading to a rapid growth of websites across universities, companies, and eventually the public.

In 1994, Tim Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), an international community that develops open standards to ensure the web remains free and accessible. The W3C continues to guide the development of core technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
