The Pressure of Social Media

censorship, cancel culture & social conformity

We've all felt pressurised by social media whether we admit it or not (or whether we've been able to identify it or not). Why is it that we let something unseen and unknown, or more specifically, someone online dictate our feelings and choices?

Social media pressure refers to the virtual pressure put on a group or an individual to behave, feel or act in a certain manner. The pressure can be negative; particularly regarding body image, financial insecurity and number of followers/likes. However, it can also be positive; justice and accountability for actions, knowledge, charity and awareness.

Negative influence increases the tendency to compare your life or appearance to others, leaving you feeling inadequate or inferior. This can lead to cyberhate, mental illness and cancel culture, while also spreading misinformation and creating panic. Positive influence increases awareness of otherwise censored topics, encourages good deeds, spreads news quickly, holds people and authorities accountable for their actions, and provides solidarity.
